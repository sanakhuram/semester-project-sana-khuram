Lets Explore Together

For Kids

Are you a young person looking to learn more about science? Come on down to our museum, there’s plenty to see and do. You can learn about Newtonian physics from our bumper swing, or why not travel back in time and meet our resident Woolly Mammoth? Our exhibits are designed to be accessible for interested minds, so make sure you come ready to learn and explore.

We also have the Young Stars club which meets once a week on a Saturday between 10:00 and 13:00 where you’ll get to explore and experiment with our team of experts. During the school holidays we run special holiday clubs where you can join other children your age to go on a journey of discovery. Each holiday we pick a new theme to explore. To find out more about the holiday club and how you can join, send us a message.

For Teachers

Our museum offers a myriad of educational resources and experiences tailored to enrich classroom learning. From expert-led guided tours that unravel the mysteries of the past to hands-on sessions in our cutting-edge learning laboratory. Additionally, our captivating video presentations are designed to captivate young imaginations and ignite a passion for exploration and discovery.

As a community-driven museum, we want to work with schools to create places of learning and exploring. Our team are on hand to give your students guided tours of the museum, teach them in our learning laboratory, and provide great video presentations that will excite and inspire them. Please get in touch with us here to find out more about our facilities and to arrange a time to bring your students through for a visit.

For Researchers

Are you looking to get involved with our team of researchers and academics? Our museum offers various ways for you to use our resources and contribute towards them. We have online records, laboratory space, and a working relationship with a number of universities around the country. Serving in a science museum allows researchers to inspire curiosity, ignite passion for discovery, and cultivate a deeper appreciation for the wonders of the natural world among visitors of all ages and backgrounds.

By becoming part of the museum's team, researchers have the opportunity to bridge the gap between scientific discovery and public understanding, translating complex concepts into accessible and engaging experiences. Through collaborative projects, researchers can leverage the museum's resources and interdisciplinary networks to explore diverse research avenues, conduct experiments, and contribute to the development of interactive exhibits and educational programs.
