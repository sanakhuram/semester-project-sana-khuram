Exhibition Spaces

Robotics and AI

Welcome to the Information Revolution, where robotics and artificial intelligence (AI) stand as the forefront of technological advancement. As we delve deeper into this era, we witness the seamless integration of AI into our daily lives, from smart home applications to the expansive industrial applications of robotics. Indeed, the future has arrived, presenting boundless possibilities and transformative innovations. The information revolution is here and robotics and artificial intelligence are the science of the future. From useful home applications of AI to industrial uses of robotics, the future is here. You can even say hello and shake the hand of Rob the Robot. An AI and robotics exhibition space serves as a platform for exploration, innovation, and dialogue, inspiring curiosity and encouraging visitors to contemplate the profound impact of AI and robotics on the way we live, work, and interact with the world.

Biology and Medicine

From micro-organisms to the human body, major breakthroughs in biology are offering us unique insights into the great wonders of the tiny world. For many people their life expectancy is much longer and their quality of life much improved, thanks to the growth of our understanding of medicine. Over a series of exhibits we explore the history of medicine and take a look at some major breakthroughs including the discovery of penicillin and the first heart transplant. Many of our great medicinal discoveries have come not only from the lab, but also from observing animals in the wild. Often our fellow creatures have beat us to it. Our main aims to educate, inspire curiosity, and raise awareness about the complexities of life sciences and healthcare, empowering visitors to make informed decisions about their health and well-being.

Evolution

For centuries, philosophers and scientists have wrestled with the question of our origins. Where do we come from and how did we get here? Since Darwin proposed his theory of evolution we have had a framework for exploring and understanding our place in this world. Discover the origins of life on this planet and how the species we know today have evolved our time. You can also take some time to meet a few of the creatures who didn’t survive, including our Woolly Mammoth, the Dodos, and a number of dinosaurs too. As we peer into the past, we gain invaluable insights into the intricate web of life, marveling at the resilience and adaptability of living organisms across epochs and continents.

Ecology Section

As we learn more and more of the impact humans are having on the planet, the more important it becomes that scientists explore how to create a sustainable future not just for humans but for the whole planet. In our ecology section of the museum, we unpack the dangers we currently face including global warming, extinction of species, and pollution. But we also focus on the positive ways we can all contribute to a brighter future. In this section, we offer a holistic perspective on sustainability, emphasizing not only the dire consequences of inaction but also the transformative power of collective action. Museums often offer educational programs and workshops related to ecology for school groups, families, and the general public. These programs may include guided tours, lectures, and demonstrations by scientists and conservationists.

Cosmology

Explore the wonders of our cosmos. Our fantastic exhibition, ‘The Sky Above Us’, explores the night sky and what we can see and know about the universe around us. We’ll locate the various constellations and galaxies that can be seen and learn a bit about the early navigators who used the stars to travel by. Follow the journey of our solar exploration: from early Arab traders, to Galileo’s telescope, to the latest exploration of the planets in our solar system.
